1. Purpose
   1.This policy establishes minimum safeguards to protect Company Data, including any electronic health information handled by <CompanyName>, in accordance with HIPAA compliance and SOC 2 attestation requirements.
   1.2 The objectives are to prevent unauthorized access, ensure service continuity, and promote responsible handling of sensitive information.

2. Scope
   2.1 Applies to all employees, contractors, and vendors who access Company systems.
   2.2 Sections marked “ePHI” apply where electronic protected health information is stored, processed, transmitted, backed up, logged containing ePHI identifiers, accessed via admin consoles, or used by mobile/endpoint clients with ePHI access.

3. Governance & Roles
   3.The Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) is responsible for developing and implementing this policy, ensuring its alignment with organizational objectives, and overseeing the security program through regular audits and updates.
   3.The Chief Compliance Officer oversees privacy practices and breach communications.
   3.3 System Owners review system access requests and conduct user access audits.
   3.4 All personnel complete training and report suspected incidents without delay.

4. Risk Management (Administrative)
   4.An enterprise risk analysis is conducted annually and following significant modifications. The outcomes are documented within an organized repository of risks, including their respective stakeholders and deadlines.
   4.The board oversees risks rated as critical or high, ensuring they are addressed within a specified timeline. Acceptance of these risks necessitates a detailed business case supported by the CISO.
   4.3 Sanctions: Policy violations shall be addressed through control activities such as audits, reviews, and remediation plans, which may include corrective actions up to termination of access rights.

5. Access Control (Technical & Administrative)
   5.1 Access is granted based on job duties and approved by the relevant manager and System Owner (least-privilege).
   5.2 Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is mandatory for administrators and remote access. (MFA for all users is recommended.)
   5.3 Sessions must lock after 20 minutes of inactivity and require re-authentication to resume.
   5.User access reviews are conducted quarterly on high-risk systems and semiannually on others; documentation of remediation is required.

6. Workforce Security & Training (Administrative)
   6.Onboarding for new hires and annual refresher training in security and privacy principles must be completed by all personnel.
   6.2 Departing personnel have access revoked within 24 hours of separation. Company assets and credentials are returned or disabled.

7. Encryption & Key Management (Technical)
   7.All production databases and file stores containing sensitive data are encrypted at rest using strong, industry-accepted algorithms through secure transmission protocols and certificate management practices.
   7.2 Data in transit should use secure protocols (TLS 1.2+) unless otherwise approved by management. Exceptions must be documented and include compensating controls.
   7.3 Keys are generated, stored, and rotated using AWS Key Management Service (KMS) for encryption standard compliance.

8. Logging & Monitoring (Technical)
   8.1 Security-relevant events (authentication results, privilege changes, configuration changes, and incident indicators) are logged centrally where feasible.
   8.2 Logs are retained for at least 365 days, with restricted access and tamper-resistant storage for critical systems; centralized logging; immutable storage for critical logs; SIEM correlation; time sync (NTP).
   8.3 The security team reviews alerts and trends and escalates potential incidents for investigation.

9. Incident Response (Administrative & Technical)
   9.1 Suspected security incidents must be reported to Security and the Privacy Officer immediately upon discovery.
   9.2 The incident team performs triage, containment, eradication, and recovery, and documents lessons learned.
   9.3 Where ePHI is involved, the Privacy Officer coordinates regulatory notifications consistent with applicable law.

10. Contingency Planning & Backups (Administrative & Technical)
   10.1 Production data is backed up regularly and encrypted in storage and transit to the backup repository; backups are tested quarterly and retained per data retention policy.
   10.Restore procedures are tested monthly; results and defects are tracked for 6 months; quarterly restore tests are conducted; annual DR testing includes recovery drills.
   10.3 Disaster recovery objectives (RTO/RPO) are defined for critical services and validated through an annual exercise.

11. Physical Safeguards
   11.1 Facilities housing production systems implement badge-based access control measures and utilize visitor management protocols for entry verification.
   11.2 Workstations used to access sensitive data employ secure screens, privacy filters for PHI, and clear desks for PHI.

12. Vendor & Third-Party Management (Organizational)
   12.1 Establish controls to meet SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria (TSC) for Security (Common Criteria), with optional categories Availability, Confidentiality, Processing Integrity, Privacy as in scope for <CompanyName>. Additionally, all uses/disclosures of this data require explicit authorization as per applicable laws, consents, or BAA agreements.
   12.2 Business Associate Agreements shall be executed prior to any vendor handling ePHI; subcontractors must comply with equivalent protections.

13. Data Classification & Handling
   13.1 Data is categorized (e.g., Public, Internal, Confidential, Restricted/ePHI). Handling rules specify storage, sharing, and disposal requirements.
   13.2 Personnel must access only the minimum data necessary to perform assigned duties.

14. Secure Development & Change Management
   14.1 Changes to production services must undergo a formal Change Management process that includes peer reviews and approvals by authorized personnel based on the potential impact and risks associated with the changes.
   14.2 Code changes undergo thorough scanning for vulnerabilities; high-risk issues are prioritized for immediate resolution or managed through an approved exception process. integrity measures include hashing/signing where applicable; change detection; anti-tamper techniques.
   14.3 Secrets must be securely stored in managed vaults; direct embedding of secrets in source code is forbidden.

15. Vulnerability & Patch Management
   15.1 Systems are scanned quarterly; findings are triaged and tracked to closure with owners and due dates.
   15.2 Critical vulnerabilities must be remediated within 14 days, and High within 30 days unless an approved exception exists.

16. Documentation & Retention
   16.1 Security and privacy program documentation, access review evidence, incident records, and training logs are retained for at least six (6) years from creation or last effective date.
   16.2 This policy is reviewed at least annually and upon material changes.

17. Privacy Program (Overview)
   17.1 Notices describe how personal information is collected, used, and shared; requests for access or deletion of personal information are handled in accordance with applicable laws.
   17.2 Data is retained only as long as needed for business and legal purposes, after which it is securely disposed of.

18. Enforcement
   18.1 Non-compliance may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination and legal remedies where applicable.
