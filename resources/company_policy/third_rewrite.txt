1. Purpose
   1.This policy establishes controls to meet SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria (TSC) for Security, with Availability, Confidentiality, Processing Integrity, and Privacy as in scope for <CompanyName>.
   1.The purpose is to establish a comprehensive framework that ensures HIPAA compliance, including Privacy, Security, and Breach Notification standards, as well as SOC 2 attestation criteria for Trust Services Criteria.

2. Scope
   2.1 Applies to all employees, contractors, and vendors who access Company systems.
   2.2 Sections marked “ePHI” apply where electronic protected health information is processed.

3. Governance & Roles
   3.The Board of Directors oversees the risk management process and approves this policy suite.
   3.2 The Privacy Officer oversees privacy practices and breach communications.
   3.3 Engineering / SDLC Owners ensure secure design, code review, CI/CD, change control, and secrets management for their systems and oversee user access reviews.
   3.4 All personnel complete training and report suspected incidents without delay.

4. Risk Management (Administrative)
   4.An enterprise risk analysis shall be conducted at least annually and after significant changes. Results shall be documented in a risk register with assigned owners and deadlines.
   4.2 Risks rated Critical or High are remediated within 7 days if they are critical, and within 14 days if they are high. High/Critical risks must be remediated within 30-90 days of identification, with written business justification and CISO approval for acceptance.
   4.3 Sanctions: policy violations may lead to corrective action up to termination.

5. Access Control (Technical & Administrative)
   5.1 Access is granted based on job duties and approved by the relevant manager and System Owner (least-privilege).
   5.2 Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is mandatory for administrators and remote access. (MFA for all users is recommended.)
   5.3 Sessions must lock after 20 minutes of inactivity and require re-authentication to resume.
   5.User access reviews are conducted twice per year on high-risk systems and at least monthly on other systems; documentation of remediation is required.

6. Workforce Security & Training (Administrative)
   6.Onboarding of new hires must occur prior to granting system access; all personnel must participate in an annual refresher course on security and privacy principles.
   6.2 Departing personnel have access revoked within 24 hours of separation. Company assets and credentials are returned or disabled.

7. Encryption & Key Management (Technical)
   7.1 All production databases and file stores containing sensitive data are encrypted at rest using strong, industry-accepted algorithms.
   7.2 Data in transit should use encryption at rest/in transit; key management; data loss prevention; secure disposal of data; NDAs for handling sensitive information.
   7.The system generates, stores, and rotates three keys as per internal key management procedures.

8. Logging & Monitoring (Technical)
   8.1 Security-relevant events (authentication results, privilege changes, configuration changes, and incident indicators) are logged centrally where feasible; tracking of these logs is maintained in a central register for easy access and reference. integrity measures such as hashing/signing and change detection are implemented.
   8.2 Logs are retained for at least 1 year online, with restricted access and tamper-resistant storage for critical systems, and include an automated system for monitoring and alerting in case of any anomalies.
   8.3 The security team reviews alerts and trends and escalates potential incidents for investigation.

9. Incident Response (Administrative & Technical)
   9.1 Suspected security incidents should be logged and maintained in a central register for review by Security and the Privacy Officer at least once per quarter.
   9.2 The incident team performs triage, containment, eradication, and recovery, and documents lessons learned.
   9.3 Where ePHI is involved, the Privacy Officer coordinates regulatory notifications consistent with applicable law.

10. Contingency Planning & Backups (Administrative & Technical)
   10.1 Production data is backed up regularly and encrypted in storage and transit to the backup repository.
   10.2 Restore procedures are annually tested; quarterly restore drills are conducted.
   10.3 Disaster recovery objectives (RTO/RPO) are defined for critical services and validated annually through an annual exercise; quarterly restores are also tested.

11. Physical Safeguards
   11.1 Facilities housing production systems use badge-controlled access and visitor sign-in procedures.
   11.2 Workstations used to access sensitive data employ full-disk encryption and automatic screen locking; patch SLAs (critical ≤ 7 days). PHI must be securely stored on these workstations and a clear desk policy applies.

12. Vendor & Third-Party Management (Organizational)
   12.3 Third-party management includes conducting thorough vetting processes for vendors handling sensitive data, ensuring compliance with established security standards.
   12.All Business Associate Agreements shall be executed prior to any vendor handling ePHI; subcontractors must comply with equivalent terms.

13. Data Classification & Handling
   13.1 Data is categorized (e.g., Public, Internal, Confidential, Restricted/ePHI). Handling rules specify storage, sharing, and disposal requirements. Additionally, least privilege principles must be followed. Additionally, least privilege principles must be followed.
   13.2 Personnel must access only the minimum data necessary to perform assigned duties.

14. Secure Development & Change Management
   14.1 Changes to production services must undergo a formal Change Management process that includes peer reviews and approvals by authorized personnel based on the potential impact and risks associated with the changes.
   14.2 Continuous scanning of code changes identifies known vulnerabilities; high-risk issues are prioritized for remediation before release or tracked with an approved exception, with exceptions requiring approval and having an expiration date.
   14.3 Secrets are stored in managed vaults; direct embedding of secrets in source code is prohibited.

15. Vulnerability & Patch Management
   15.1 Systems are scanned regularly; findings are triaged and tracked to closure.
   15.The high-risk exceptions must be approved by the executive before they can proceed beyond the initial 90-day period; otherwise, the vulnerability remains critical.

16. Documentation & Retention
   16.1 Security and privacy program documentation, BAA inventory, access review exports, SIEM dashboards, incident records, backup test logs, DR reports, and training rosters are retained for at least six (6) years from creation or last effective date. Additionally, categorized and handled according to established procedures.
   16.2 This policy is reviewed at least annually and upon material changes.

17. Privacy Program (Overview)
   17.1 Notices describe how personal information is collected, used, and shared; requests for access or deletion of personal information are handled in accordance with applicable laws. Personal information shall be protected according to privacy regulations.
   17.2 Backups: Encrypt; test quarterly; retain per data retention policy; use AWS Key Management Service (KMS) for encryption; rotate keys every 3 years; manage secrets using AWS Secrets Manager. use AWS Key Management Service (KMS) for encryption; rotate keys every 3 years; manage secrets using AWS Secrets Manager.

18. Enforcement
   18.1 Non-compliance may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination and legal remedies where applicable.
